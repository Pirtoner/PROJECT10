Knives Out

A Whodunit Masterclass

The Mystery of the Thrombeys

Knives Out is a modern take on the classic "Whodunit" mystery. When the wealthy crime novelist Harlan Thrombey is found dead in his estate just after his 85th birthday, everyone in his eccentric family becomes a suspect.

The plot follows the brilliant detective Benoit Blanc as he untangles a web of lies, greed, and family secrets. Unlike traditional mysteries, this film flips the script by showing us part of the truth early on, then challenging us to see if the "good heart" of one person can survive a house full of "knives out."

"A detective at the scene of a crime, is like a conductor in front of an orchestra." — Benoit Blanc

Favourite Character: Benoit Blanc (Detective)

Why Benoit Blanc is my Favourite Character

  • His Unique Methods
    • The "Donut Hole" Theory: Finding the center of the mystery.
    • Observation: Watching how characters react to stress.
  • His Moral Values
    • Protection: He looks out for those who are innocent.
    • Honesty: He follows the truth, no matter where it leads.

Life Lesson: The Power of Integrity

The biggest lesson I learned from this movie is that kindness is not a weakness. While every other character is trying to trick each other to get the inheritance, Marta Cabrera succeeds simply because she is a good person who cares about others.
